# Competitive Research — Sources and Method

## Objective

Map what competitors are doing in paid advertising to identify:
1. Saturated claims (to avoid)
2. Uncovered angles (white spaces)
3. Market sophistication level
4. Formats that perform in the category

---

## Research Sources

### 1. Meta Ad Library (Primary Source)
**URL:** facebook.com/ads/library
**Filter:** Country = target market + Category = All ads

**What to analyze:**
- Number of active ads (volume = budget = confidence in what works)
- How long ads have been running (longer = better performance)
- Dominant formats (video / static / carousel)
- Recurring claims in headlines
- Entry angle (pain / proof / education / identity)
- Awareness stage targeted

**Strong signal:** An ad that has been running for 30+ days with high impression volume = confirmed winner.

---

### 2. Competitor Websites — Copywriting Analysis
**Pages to read:** Hero section, product pages, FAQ, About page

**What to extract:**
- Main claim (first H1 sentence)
- Unique mechanism highlighted
- Social proof used (numbers, logos, testimonials)
- Tone and language register
- Objections addressed in FAQ

---

### 3. Review Platforms
**Trustpilot, Google Reviews, Amazon:** Read reviews of direct competitors.

**What to extract:**
- Unfulfilled promises (negative reviews) = potential differentiating angle
- What customers genuinely love (positive reviews) = credible claims in the category
- Recurring complaints = unresolved pain points you can address

---

### 4. TikTok + Instagram — Organic
**Why:** Organic content that performs foreshadows ad angles.

**Technique:** Search the product category on TikTok. Filter by "most popular." Observe the hooks of the top 10 videos.

---

### 5. Google (Intent Search)
Type product keywords to see:
- Google ads at the top of the page (search intent angle = awareness Level 4-5)
- Comparisons and "alternative to X" in organic results
- "People also ask" questions = common objections and confusions

## White Space Criteria

A white space is an available position when:
1. No competitor addresses this audience sub-segment
2. No competitor uses this angle (e.g., everyone uses proof, nobody uses education)
3. A strong claim is present in customer reviews but absent in category ads
4. An entire awareness stage is not covered (e.g., nobody talks to the Unaware)

**Rule:** A white space is only valid if you can credibly and durably claim it.
