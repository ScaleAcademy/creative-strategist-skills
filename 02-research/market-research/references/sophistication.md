# Market Sophistication — Eugene Schwartz

## The 5 Levels

### Level 1 — First In Market
No one has made this claim before. State it directly and boldly.
> Creative implication: Simple claim-first ads work. No mechanism or proof layer needed yet.

### Level 2 — Competition Emerges
Similar claims exist. Amplify — add specificity, numbers, timeframes.
> Creative implication: Add proof points. "Lose 10kg in 30 days" beats "lose weight."

### Level 3 — Mechanism Phase
Claims are equal across competitors. Introduce your unique mechanism — HOW you deliver the result.
> Creative implication: Lead with the unique method, ingredient, or process. The claim becomes secondary.

### Level 4 — Mechanism Saturation
Everyone has a unique mechanism. Show yours is superior.
> Creative implication: Compare mechanisms. "Unlike X method, ours does Y."

### Level 5 — Hyper-Sophisticated
Every claim and mechanism has been heard. Shift to persona identification and identity.
> Creative implication: Lead with identity, lifestyle, community. The product is almost irrelevant in the hook.

---

## Diagnosis Questions
1. What claims dominate competitor ads? Are they all saying the same thing?
2. Are competitors leading with mechanisms or just claims?
3. Are any competitors leading with identity/lifestyle rather than the product?
4. What level of proof does the audience seem to demand?

## AutoTrades Context
The trading/signal community market in France is currently at **Level 1–2**. Simple promise-first messaging still works. Window of opportunity to build creative infrastructure before the market sophisticates.
