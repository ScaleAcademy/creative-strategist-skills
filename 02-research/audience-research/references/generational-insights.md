# Generational Insights — Advertising Context

Quick reference for generational characteristics relevant to ad creative and targeting. Use this to calibrate tone, format, and messaging for the dominant generation in your audience.

**Usage rule:** Generations are broad strokes, not absolutes. Use these as starting defaults, then refine with actual persona data from audience research.

---

## Generation Overview (2026)

| Generation | Born | Age in 2026 | Population share (approx.) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Gen Z | 1997-2012 | 14-29 | Growing purchasing power |
| Millennials | 1981-1996 | 30-45 | Peak earning years |
| Gen X | 1965-1980 | 46-61 | High disposable income |
| Boomers | 1946-1964 | 62-80 | Established wealth, retiring |

---

## Gen Z (14-29)

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Media consumption** | TikTok (primary), Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts, Snapchat. Short-form video dominant. Desktop is secondary -- mobile-first always. |
| **Communication style** | Visual-first, meme-literate, ironic humor, authenticity over polish. Skeptical of corporate messaging. |
| **Values** | Authenticity, social justice, mental health awareness, individuality, sustainability (but price-sensitive). |
| **Ad format preferences** | UGC, native-looking content, creator partnerships, memes, short video (<15s). Hates anything that looks like a traditional ad. |
| **Tone that works** | Casual, self-aware, slightly ironic, honest about imperfections. "We know this is an ad, but..." |
| **Tone that fails** | Corporate, preachy, overly polished, "fellow kids" energy, fake urgency. |
| **Purchase triggers** | Peer validation, creator endorsement, FOMO, identity alignment, TikTok trends. |
| **LF8 emphasis** | LF8 Social Approval, LF4 Attraction, LF6 Status (through uniqueness, not traditional markers). |

---

## Millennials (30-45)

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Media consumption** | Instagram (primary), YouTube, Facebook (declining but still used), TikTok (growing), podcasts. Mix of short and long-form. |
| **Communication style** | Value-driven, story-oriented, appreciate transparency. Want to understand "why" before "what." |
| **Values** | Experiences over things, work-life balance, personal development, family (many are parents now), financial security. |
| **Ad format preferences** | Video testimonials, founder stories, educational content, carousels, email. Open to longer content if valuable. |
| **Tone that works** | Aspirational but grounded, empathetic, expert but approachable. Show understanding of their life stage. |
| **Tone that fails** | Condescending, too youthful/trendy, ignoring their responsibilities (they have mortgages and kids now). |
| **Purchase triggers** | Social proof (reviews, case studies), convenience, ROI clarity, risk reduction (guarantees), expert endorsement. |
| **LF8 emphasis** | LF5 Comfortable Living, LF7 Care of Loved Ones, LF6 Status (career advancement, financial). |

---

## Gen X (46-61)

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Media consumption** | Facebook (primary social), YouTube, email newsletters, Google Search, LinkedIn (professional). Traditional media still relevant. |
| **Communication style** | Direct, no-nonsense, skeptical of hype. Appreciate substance over style. Want facts and proof. |
| **Values** | Self-reliance, pragmatism, financial stability, family security, quality over quantity. |
| **Ad format preferences** | Long-form content, detailed product comparisons, case studies, expert reviews, email. Less responsive to short-form video. |
| **Tone that works** | Professional, straightforward, data-backed, respectful of their intelligence and experience. |
| **Tone that fails** | Trendy slang, excessive emojis, oversimplified messaging, anything that feels like it's trying too hard. |
| **Purchase triggers** | Product quality, value for money, expert authority, detailed specs, customer service reputation. |
| **LF8 emphasis** | LF3 Security, LF7 Care of Loved Ones, LF1 Health (increasingly relevant). |

---

## Boomers (62-80)

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Media consumption** | Facebook (dominant social), email, Google Search, YouTube, traditional media (TV, radio, print). Very low TikTok/Instagram usage. |
| **Communication style** | Formal, appreciates detail, reads long copy, responds to authority and credentials. |
| **Values** | Loyalty, tradition, financial security, health, family legacy, established brands. |
| **Ad format preferences** | Long-form text, detailed emails, video testimonials from peers, before/after proof. Carousels with clear text. |
| **Tone that works** | Respectful, clear, professional, authority-led. Acknowledge their experience and wisdom. |
| **Tone that fails** | Youth-oriented slang, fast-paced editing, assumption of tech-savviness, dismissive of their generation. |
| **Purchase triggers** | Trust (brand reputation), authority (expert endorsement), fear of loss (health, financial), family benefit. |
| **LF8 emphasis** | LF1 Health/Longevity, LF3 Security, LF7 Care of Loved Ones. |

## Generation x Ad Angle

| Angle | Gen Z | Millennials | Gen X | Boomers |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| C1 Problem | Works if relatable and specific | Strong -- they feel overwhelmed | Strong -- practical pain points | Moderate -- health/financial pain |
| C2 Result | Needs social proof from peers | Strong -- show the transformation | Very strong -- show the ROI | Strong -- show the proof |
| C3 Education | Short-form, visual, entertaining | Loves it -- wants to learn | Appreciates depth and expertise | Values authority-led education |
| C4 Identity | Very strong -- identity is everything | Moderate -- values-based identity | Weak -- skeptical of tribe marketing | Weak -- prefers product focus |

## Creative Production Implications

| Decision | Gen Z default | Millennial default | Gen X default | Boomer default |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Video length | 6-15s | 15-45s | 30-90s | 60s+ |
| Production quality | Low (native) | Medium (polished but real) | Medium-high | High (professional) |
| Text amount | Minimal | Moderate | Detailed | Comprehensive |
| Humor | Ironic, meme-based | Relatable, warm | Dry, subtle | Clean, straightforward |
| CTA style | Soft, embedded | Clear but not pushy | Direct and logical | Very clear, simple steps |
