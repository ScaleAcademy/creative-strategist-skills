# Life Force 8 — Drew Eric Whitman (Ca$hvertising)

## The 8 Innate Desires

These 8 desires are biologically programmed. They are not learned — they are present in all humans, across all cultures. An ad that touches one of them bypasses the rational filter.

---

### LF1 — Survival, longevity, health
**Desire:** To live long, stay healthy, avoid physical pain.
**Opposite fear:** Disease, premature death, physical decline.
**Paid social application:** Health, fitness, supplements, medical, safety.
**Example hook:** "What people who live to 90 in good health actually do."

---

### LF2 — Food and drink
**Desire:** To enjoy good meals, satisfy hunger.
**Paid social application:** Food & beverage, restaurants, delivery, cooking.
**Example hook:** "The most satisfying meal you can make in 10 minutes."

---

### LF3 — Freedom from fear, pain, danger
**Desire:** To feel safe, protected, sheltered from threat.
**Opposite fear:** Financial, physical, or emotional threat.
**Paid social application:** Finance, insurance, home security, B2B (business risk).
**Example hook:** "The one thing that can ruin your business overnight — and how to prevent it."

---

### LF4 — Sexual companionship
**Desire:** Attraction, seduction, being desired.
**Paid social application:** Fashion, beauty, fragrance, dating, aesthetic fitness.
**Example hook:** "What people notice first when you walk into a room."

---

### LF5 — Comfortable living conditions
**Desire:** Comfort, ease, convenience, less effort for the same results.
**Paid social application:** Tech, home, productivity, tools, SaaS.
**Example hook:** "I stopped doing [task] manually. Here's why."

---

### LF6 — Superiority, winning, feeling important
**Desire:** To be recognized, admired, have status, win.
**Paid social application:** Luxury, performance, business, finance, elite training.
**Example hook:** "What the top 1% do that they don't share publicly."

---

### LF7 — Care of loved ones (children, family)
**Desire:** To protect and nurture those you love.
**Paid social application:** Life insurance, education, family products, investing.
**Example hook:** "What I put in place so my children never go without."

---

### LF8 — Social approval
**Desire:** To be loved, respected, accepted by your group.
**Opposite fear:** Rejection, shame, judgment from others.
**Paid social application:** Fashion, lifestyle, training, community, anything visible.
**Example hook:** "Successful people all have this in common — and you can see it immediately."

---

## How to Use This Reference

**Step 1:** Identify the dominant LF(s) for your product.
**Step 2:** Identify the opposite fear (what the prospect wants to avoid).
**Step 3:** Build the hook around the desire OR the fear — never both at once.

**Rule:** A product can touch several LFs, but each creative should only activate one. Dilution destroys impact.
