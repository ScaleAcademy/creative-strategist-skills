# Brand Alignment Reference

## When to Use
At the start of every client engagement and before each new sprint. Alignment decays — revisit quarterly minimum.

## Go Here
Founder or marketing lead directly. Dashboard data alone doesn't tell you the business context.

## Look For
- **Business stage signal:** validating = need proof of concept; scaling = need volume + consistency; optimizing = need efficiency + LTV
- **Media buyer signal:** what is the algorithm actually optimizing for? This determines what creative success looks like
- **Produced vs. planned gap:** difference between the creative plan and what actually shipped — reveals execution constraints
- **LTV context:** if LTV is high, CPA tolerance is higher — don't optimize for cheap leads if the buyer knows they're worth 3× more

## Why This Matters
Creative strategy that optimizes for the wrong metric wastes budget. A brand scaling fast needs high-volume testing. A brand optimizing needs iteration on winners. A brand validating needs simple, cheap-to-test concepts first.

## Growth Stage → Creative Implication

| Stage | Creative Priority |
|---|---|
| Validating | Simple concepts, low production cost, rapid iteration |
| Scaling | Volume + consistency, build winner library, diverse angles |
| Optimizing | Deep iteration on winners, LTV-focused creative, retention angles |
