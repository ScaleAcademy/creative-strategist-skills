# Brand Guidelines Checklist

## When to Use
Before any creative work on a new brand or client. If `brand-context.md` doesn't exist → run brand-guidelines first.

## Go Here
Brand website (About, Product, FAQ), Meta Ad Library, review platforms (Trustpilot, Amazon), founder LinkedIn, press mentions.

## Look For
- Unique mechanism: what the brand claims only they can do
- Voice signals: how they open sentences, what words recur, what they avoid
- Constraint signals: regulated category (health claims, finance), family-safe tone, before/after restrictions on Meta
- Audience signals: who appears in imagery, what pain points surface in copy

## Why This Matters
The brand context document is the single source of truth for all downstream work. A weak brief produces weak creative. Gaps here cost hours downstream.

## Confirmed vs. Inferred Constraints

| Type | Definition |
|---|---|
| Confirmed | Explicitly stated on site or in brand docs |
| Inferred | Reasonable assumption from category, tone, or Meta policies |

Always label which is which. Never present an inference as a confirmed fact.

## Common Research Gaps
- Pricing (often hidden) — check multiple pages + Trustpilot
- Actual differentiator vs. claimed differentiator (what customers actually value)
- Meta Ad Library — look at what they're *currently* running vs. what they claim
