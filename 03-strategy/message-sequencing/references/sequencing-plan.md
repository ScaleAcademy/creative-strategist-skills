# Message Sequencing Plan — TOF / MOF / BOF / Re-engage

A 4-touchpoint framework for sequencing creative messages across the funnel. Each stage has a distinct objective, audience awareness level, and creative approach.

**Usage rule:** Build the full sequence BEFORE producing individual creatives. Each touchpoint must advance the prospect to the next awareness level — not repeat the same message louder.

---

## Stage 1 — TOF (Top of Funnel)

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Objective** | Interrupt, educate, build problem awareness. Make the prospect recognize their pain or desire. |
| **Awareness level** | Unaware → Problem Aware |
| **Recommended angles** | C1 Problem (soft — name it, don't sell), C3 Education (teach something useful), C4 Identity (for sophisticated markets) |
| **Recommended formats** | F45 Yapper, F16 How-To, F20 Native Text Overlay, F30 Selfie/POV, F32 Skit, F18 Listicle |
| **Hook direction** | Pattern interrupt, curiosity gap, identity callout, empathy. No product mention in the hook. |
| **CTA type** | Soft or none. "Learn more" / "Watch the full breakdown" / No CTA (content-first) |
| **Budget allocation** | 50-60% of total creative budget |
| **Key metric** | Hook Rate (3s views / impressions), Thumb-Stop Rate, CPM |
| **Creative volume** | Highest volume here — test 3-5 new creatives per week minimum |

**Creative rules:**
- Do NOT mention the product in the first 5 seconds
- Lead with the problem, the story, or the insight
- The ad should feel like organic content, not an advertisement
- Prioritize authenticity over production quality
- Test multiple hooks on the same body — hooks are the variable at TOF

---

## Stage 2 — MOF (Middle of Funnel)

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Objective** | Build trust, differentiate, prove the mechanism. Move from "I have a problem" to "This solution makes sense." |
| **Awareness level** | Problem Aware → Solution Aware → Product Aware |
| **Recommended angles** | C2 Result (case studies, proof), C3 Education (mechanism, how it works), C1 Problem (deeper pain, objection handling) |
| **Recommended formats** | F06 Case Study, F11 Expert Explainer, F25 Podcast, F10 Demo, F03 Before/After, F36 Testimonial |
| **Hook direction** | Social proof, authority, bold claim, transformation. Can reference the problem from TOF. |
| **CTA type** | Medium commitment. "See how it works" / "Book a free call" / "Try it free for 14 days" |
| **Budget allocation** | 20-30% of total creative budget |
| **Key metric** | CTR, Landing Page View Rate, CPC |
| **Creative volume** | 2-3 new creatives per week. More targeted, higher production value than TOF. |

**Creative rules:**
- The product can appear, but framed as proof — not as the hero
- Lead with a result, a testimonial, or a mechanism explanation
- Every MOF creative must answer one specific objection or doubt
- Use retargeting audiences: people who engaged with TOF content
- The prospect should think "This could work for me" after watching

---

## Stage 3 — BOF (Bottom of Funnel)

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Objective** | Convert. Remove the last barrier. Make the decision feel safe and urgent. |
| **Awareness level** | Product Aware → Most Aware |
| **Recommended angles** | C2 Result (direct offer, social proof volume), C1 Problem (cost of inaction, urgency), C4 Identity (community, belonging) |
| **Recommended formats** | F05 Billboard, F29 Review, F33 Social Proof Mashup, F44 VSL, F27 Press, F26 Post-It |
| **Hook direction** | Urgency, risk reversal, price anchor, FOMO, direct offer. Product name in the hook is fine. |
| **CTA type** | Direct and specific. "Join now — 6 spots left" / "Buy now — 30-day guarantee" / "Claim your discount" |
| **Budget allocation** | 10-15% of total creative budget |
| **Key metric** | CPA, ROAS, Conversion Rate |
| **Creative volume** | 1-2 new creatives per week. Focused on offer variations, not new angles. |

**Creative rules:**
- The product IS the hero — no need to educate anymore
- Lead with the offer, the guarantee, or the social proof
- Scarcity must be real (cohort limits, seasonal offers, genuine stock limits)
- Address the #1 remaining objection head-on
- Retarget: landing page visitors, add-to-carts, video viewers 75%+

---

## Stage 4 — Re-engage

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Objective** | Win back lapsed prospects and past customers. Reactivate interest with new information or urgency. |
| **Awareness level** | Most Aware (they know you, they just didn't buy — or bought before) |
| **Recommended angles** | C2 Result (new results, updates), C1 Problem (what they've missed), C4 Identity (community FOMO) |
| **Recommended formats** | F09 Comment Response, F17 Letter, F26 Post-It, F05 Billboard, F22 Notes App |
| **Hook direction** | "We've updated...", "You left something behind...", "Since you last visited...", "Last chance..." |
| **CTA type** | Re-activation. "Come back — we've added X" / "Your results are waiting" / "Last chance: offer ends tonight" |
| **Budget allocation** | 5-10% of total creative budget |
| **Key metric** | Reactivation Rate, CPA on re-engaged segment, LTV |
| **Creative volume** | 1 new creative per week. Rotate based on time since last interaction. |

**Creative rules:**
- Reference their previous interaction if possible ("You watched our video about X...")
- New information is the #1 re-engagement lever — show what's changed
- Time-based sequences: Day 3, Day 7, Day 14, Day 30 after last interaction
- Separate past customers (upsell) from non-converters (win-back)

## Full Sequence Overview

| Stage | Awareness | Angle | Format Priority | CTA | Budget |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| TOF | Unaware → Problem | C3, C1 (soft), C4 | Yapper, How-To, POV | Soft / None | 50-60% |
| MOF | Problem → Product | C2, C3, C1 | Case Study, Demo, Testimonial | Medium | 20-30% |
| BOF | Product → Most | C2, C1 (urgency), C4 | Billboard, Mashup, VSL | Direct | 10-15% |
| Re-engage | Most Aware | C2, C1, C4 | Letter, Post-It, Comment | Re-activate | 5-10% |

---

## Awareness Progression Logic

```
TOF creative → prospect becomes Problem Aware
        ↓
MOF creative → prospect becomes Solution/Product Aware
        ↓
BOF creative → prospect becomes Most Aware → converts
        ↓
Re-engage → lapsed prospect re-enters at MOF or BOF
```

**Critical rule:** Never skip stages. An Unaware prospect exposed to a BOF ad will not convert — they'll ignore it. The sequence must be respected.

---

## Testing Cadence

| Stage | New creatives/week | Test variable | Kill rule |
|---|---|---|---|
| TOF | 3-5 | Hooks (same body, different hooks) | Hook Rate < 20% after 1,000 impressions |
| MOF | 2-3 | Angle + proof type | CTR < 1.5% after 500 clicks |
| BOF | 1-2 | Offer + CTA variation | CPA > 1.5x target after €100 spend |
| Re-engage | 1 | Timing + message | Reactivation < 2% after 7 days |
