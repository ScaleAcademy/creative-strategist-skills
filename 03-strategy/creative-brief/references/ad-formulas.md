# Ad Copy Formulas

Proven copywriting structures for ad scripts and body copy. Each formula provides a sequence for organizing the argument — from hook to CTA.

**Usage rule:** Choose the formula AFTER defining the awareness stage and angle. The formula is the skeleton; the angle and hook are the flesh.

---

## The 7 Formulas

### PAS — Problem, Agitation, Solution

**Structure:**
1. **Problem** — Name the pain clearly and specifically
2. **Agitation** — Amplify it. Show consequences, project into the future, make inaction painful
3. **Solution** — Present the product as the exit from that pain

**When to use:** Problem Aware audiences. C1 angle. When the prospect knows the pain but hasn't acted.
**Best format:** Video (Yapper, UGC, Founder), Static (Headline + subhead)
**C1-C4 pairing:** C1 Problem (primary), C3 Education (secondary)

**Example:**
> You spend 3 hours briefing ads that nobody watches past second 2. (Problem)
> Every week, same cycle — brief, produce, launch, fail. Your budget bleeds while your competitors scale. (Agitation)
> This creative testing system eliminates guesswork. Brief → test → scale in 48 hours. (Solution)

---

### BAB — Before, After, Bridge

**Structure:**
1. **Before** — Describe the prospect's current painful reality
2. **After** — Paint the desired state vividly
3. **Bridge** — The product is the bridge between the two

**When to use:** Problem Aware → Solution Aware. C1 or C2 angle. When the transformation story is strong.
**Best format:** Video (Before/After, Testimonial, Founder), Carousel (slide progression)
**C1-C4 pairing:** C2 Result (primary), C1 Problem (secondary)

**Example:**
> Before: Launching campaigns blind, burning €3k/month with no creative system. (Before)
> After: Testing 10 creatives/week, scaling winners within 72 hours, sleeping well at night. (After)
> The bridge? A framework that turns any brief into a testable hypothesis. (Bridge)

---

### FAB — Feature, Advantage, Benefit

**Structure:**
1. **Feature** — What the product has or does (factual)
2. **Advantage** — Why that feature matters vs. alternatives
3. **Benefit** — What it means for the prospect's life

**When to use:** Solution Aware → Product Aware. C2 angle. When comparing against alternatives.
**Best format:** Static (Feature callout), Video (Demo, Expert Explainer), Carousel
**C1-C4 pairing:** C2 Result (primary), C3 Education (secondary)

**Example:**
> Feature: AI-powered creative scoring before you spend a single euro.
> Advantage: Unlike manual testing, you know which creative will win before launch.
> Benefit: You stop wasting budget on losers and scale winners from day one.

---

### AIDA — Attention, Interest, Desire, Action

**Structure:**
1. **Attention** — Stop the scroll with a pattern interrupt or bold claim
2. **Interest** — Provide a surprising fact, story, or mechanism
3. **Desire** — Paint the outcome, use social proof, make them want it
4. **Action** — Clear, specific CTA

**When to use:** All awareness stages (adapt the A and I sections). Full funnel versatile formula.
**Best format:** Video (VSL, Yapper, Founder), Long-form static, Carousel
**C1-C4 pairing:** Works with all angles — the most flexible formula

**Example:**
> 47 seconds. That's how long you have before your prospect's attention is gone. (Attention)
> We tested 2,000 hooks across 140 brands. The pattern that emerged will change how you write ads. (Interest)
> Imagine knowing, before you spend a euro, which hook will stop the scroll. (Desire)
> Get the framework. Link in bio. (Action)

---

### OCR — Objection, Counter, Resolution

**Structure:**
1. **Objection** — State the prospect's main reason for NOT buying
2. **Counter** — Dismantle the objection with logic, proof, or reframe
3. **Resolution** — Show that the path is clear now that the objection is removed

**When to use:** Product Aware → Most Aware. BOF retargeting. When the prospect is stuck on a specific doubt.
**Best format:** Video (FAQ, Founder, Comment Response), Static (objection headline)
**C1-C4 pairing:** C2 Result (primary), C1 Problem (secondary)

**Example:**
> "I don't have time to test 10 creatives a week." (Objection)
> Neither did we — until we built a system that turns one brief into 5 variations in 20 minutes. (Counter)
> Time isn't the problem. Process is. And now you have one. (Resolution)

---

### 4Ps — Promise, Picture, Proof, Push

**Structure:**
1. **Promise** — Make a bold, specific claim
2. **Picture** — Help them visualize having the result
3. **Proof** — Back it with evidence (numbers, testimonials, case study)
4. **Push** — Create urgency and CTA

**When to use:** Solution Aware → Most Aware. C2 angle. Launch sequences, offer-driven campaigns.
**Best format:** Video (VSL, Social Proof Mashup), Carousel (progressive reveal), Static (offer)
**C1-C4 pairing:** C2 Result (primary), C4 Identity (secondary)

**Example:**
> Scale your ad account past €100k/month without hiring an agency. (Promise)
> Picture this: Monday morning, your dashboard shows 3 winning creatives from last week's tests. (Picture)
> 340 brands already use this system. Average ROAS improvement: 2.3x in 60 days. (Proof)
> April cohort closes Friday. 6 spots left. (Push)

---

### Star-Story-Solution

**Structure:**
1. **Star** — Introduce a relatable character (you, a client, the prospect themselves)
2. **Story** — Tell the struggle, the turning point, the journey
3. **Solution** — The product is what made the transformation possible

**When to use:** Unaware → Problem Aware. C1 or C3 angle. When storytelling builds trust before selling.
**Best format:** Video (Founder, Personal Story, Testimonial), Long-form ad copy
**C1-C4 pairing:** C1 Problem (primary), C4 Identity (secondary)

**Example:**
> 2 years ago, I was a freelance media buyer burning out on 6 clients. (Star)
> I was working 60-hour weeks, manually testing creatives, and still losing to agencies with bigger budgets. Then I discovered something: the problem wasn't my budget — it was my creative process. (Story)
> I built a system that tests hypotheses, not guesses. Today I manage half the clients at 3x the results. (Solution)
