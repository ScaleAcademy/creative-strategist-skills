# Sound Design — Music, VO, SFX Guide

Sound is 50% of a video ad's impact. The right music choice, voice-over approach, and sound effects create emotional resonance that visuals alone cannot achieve.

---

## Music Selection by Ad Format

| Format | Genre/Style | BPM Range | Energy Level | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| F45 Yapper | None or minimal lo-fi | — | Low background | Music must not compete with voice. Silence often works better. |
| F06 Case Study | Corporate ambient, light piano | 70-90 | Low-medium | Subtle, professional. Music supports but never leads. |
| F08 Cinematic B-Roll | Cinematic orchestral, epic builds | 80-120 | Medium-high | Music IS the content. Choose tracks with clear build + peak structure. |
| F10 Demo | Tech/electronic, upbeat | 100-130 | Medium | Modern, clean. Matches the precision of a product demo. |
| F13 Founder | Acoustic, indie, warm | 80-110 | Low-medium | Human, honest feel. No corporate polish. |
| F16 How-To | Light electronic, lo-fi hip hop | 85-100 | Low | Background texture. Must not distract from learning. |
| F32 Skit | Trending audio, comedy stingers | Variable | Matches scene | Use recognizable sounds for comedy beats. Silence before punchline. |
| F33 Social Proof Mashup | Upbeat pop, motivational | 110-140 | High | Energy drives the rapid testimonial cuts. Music creates momentum. |
| F36 Testimonial | Warm ambient, soft acoustic | 70-90 | Low | The person's voice is primary. Music is emotional wallpaper. |
| F44 VSL | Builds over time (ambient → epic) | 80-120 | Low → High | Music mirrors the argument arc: problem (low) → solution (building) → CTA (peak). |

## BPM Guide by Pacing Intent

| Intent | BPM Range | Feel |
|---|---|---|
| Calm, trust-building | 60-80 | Slow, breathing, contemplative |
| Conversational, relatable | 80-100 | Natural speech rhythm, comfortable |
| Energetic, motivational | 100-120 | Forward momentum, confidence |
| Urgent, high-energy | 120-140 | Driving, demanding attention |
| Intense, dramatic | 140+ | Rare in ads — use only for pattern interrupt moments |

## Voice-Over vs Music vs Silence

### When to Use Voice-Over (VO)

| Scenario | VO approach |
|---|---|
| Educational content (How-To, Explainer) | Clear, authoritative, medium pace. VO leads, music supports. |
| Founder/personal story | Authentic, slightly imperfect. Sounds like talking to a friend. |
| Problem agitation | Slower, lower pitch. Let pauses create discomfort. |
| CTA delivery | Slightly faster, higher energy. Confident, not pushy. |
| AI-generated video (SCOTT, etc.) | ElevenLabs or similar. Choose a voice that matches character personality. |

### When to Use Music Only (No VO)

| Scenario | Music approach |
|---|---|
| Cinematic B-Roll | Music carries the entire emotional journey. Track selection is critical. |
| Before/After reveals | Let the visual contrast speak. Music provides emotional punctuation. |
| Product beauty shots | Aspirational music. The product is the star. |
| Text-overlay videos | Music provides rhythm for text animation. No VO needed. |

### When to Use Silence

| Scenario | Silence approach |
|---|---|
| Pattern interrupt (first 0.5-1s) | Dead silence in a noisy feed = attention. Break it with a strong first word. |
| Before a key reveal | Music drops out 0.5s before the payoff. Silence = anticipation. |
| Emotional peak | Let the viewer process. Silence after a powerful statement > music swell. |
| Comedy beat | The pause before the punchline. Silence creates comedic timing. |

## Sound Effects (SFX) Library

### Essential SFX Categories

| SFX Type | Use Case | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Notification/Ding** | Drawing attention to a text overlay or stat | Phone notification, cash register ding, achievement unlock |
| **Whoosh/Swipe** | Transitions between scenes or sections | Swipe sound, wind pass, slide transition |
| **Impact/Hit** | Emphasizing a key word or number | Bass drop, thud, boom, snap |
| **UI Sounds** | Screen recordings, app demos | Click, tap, typing, scroll |
| **Ambient** | Setting the scene, creating atmosphere | Office sounds, street noise, nature |
| **Comedy Stingers** | Skit/meme punchlines | Record scratch, sad trombone, vine boom |
| **Success Sounds** | Results, achievements, transformations | Level-up chime, confetti pop, applause |

### SFX Rules

- Maximum 3-4 SFX per 15-second video. More = chaotic.
- SFX must sync precisely with visual events (within 50ms).
- SFX volume: below VO, at or above music level for impact moments.
- Avoid overused TikTok sounds unless intentionally referencing the trend.

## Audio Mixing Quick Reference

| Element | Volume Level (relative) |
|---|---|
| Voice-over | 0 dB (reference level) |
| Music (under VO) | -12 to -18 dB |
| Music (solo, no VO) | -6 to -3 dB |
| SFX (emphasis) | -6 to -3 dB |
| SFX (ambient) | -18 to -24 dB |
| Silence (intentional) | -inf (true silence or room tone at -30 dB) |
