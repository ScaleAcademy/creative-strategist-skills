# Scene Structure — Duration, Camera, Motion, Lighting

Scene-by-scene blueprint for video ad production. Each scene type has defined parameters for duration, camera angle, movement, and lighting to ensure consistency and intentional visual storytelling.

---

## Scene Types

### Scene A — Hook Scene
**Purpose:** Stop the scroll. Grab attention in the first 1-3 seconds.

| Parameter | Options |
|---|---|
| **Duration** | 1-3 seconds |
| **Camera angle** | Close-up (face), Extreme close-up (eyes/hands/product), Low angle (power), Dutch angle (tension) |
| **Camera movement** | Push-in (toward subject), Handheld shake (energy/urgency), Snap zoom, Static with subject movement |
| **Lighting** | High contrast (dramatic), Natural light (authentic), Rim light (separation from background) |
| **Mood** | Urgency, curiosity, disruption, intimacy |

**Production notes:**
- Never start with a wide shot — too much information, not enough focus
- Subject should already be mid-action or mid-expression
- If using text overlay, leave visual space in the top third

---

### Scene B — Problem Scene
**Purpose:** Show or describe the pain. Make the viewer feel understood.

| Parameter | Options |
|---|---|
| **Duration** | 3-8 seconds |
| **Camera angle** | Medium shot (relatable, everyday), Over-the-shoulder (POV of the problem), Close-up on pain point (screen, face, object) |
| **Camera movement** | Static or slow pan (let the viewer absorb), Handheld (stress/chaos feel), Slight push-in (building tension) |
| **Lighting** | Flat/neutral (mundane reality), Slightly underlit (discomfort), Cool tones (clinical, detached) |
| **Mood** | Frustration, exhaustion, confusion, isolation |

**Production notes:**
- Show the specific situation, not abstract pain
- Include environmental details that the persona recognizes (messy desk, loading dashboard, empty notification screen)
- Slower pacing than hook — let the pain breathe

---

### Scene C — Agitation/Tension Scene
**Purpose:** Amplify the problem. Make inaction feel costly.

| Parameter | Options |
|---|---|
| **Duration** | 3-6 seconds |
| **Camera angle** | Close-up (tight on face/reaction), Tracking shot (following the subject through their struggle), High angle (subject feels small/overwhelmed) |
| **Camera movement** | Accelerating push-in (closing in), Handheld with increasing instability, Quick cuts between problem elements |
| **Lighting** | Darkening or shifting cooler, Shadows increasing, Harsh overhead (interrogation feel) |
| **Mood** | Mounting pressure, worry, urgency, realization |

**Production notes:**
- Cut rhythm accelerates through this scene
- Sound design matters: clock ticking, notification sounds stacking, heartbeat
- This scene bridges pain → solution. End it on a pivot moment.

---

### Scene D — Solution/Reveal Scene
**Purpose:** Introduce the solution. Shift the energy from problem to possibility.

| Parameter | Options |
|---|---|
| **Duration** | 5-12 seconds |
| **Camera angle** | Medium-wide (context + product), Product close-up (detail), Straight-on (direct, honest) |
| **Camera movement** | Reveal pull-back (from detail to context), Smooth dolly or slider (quality/premium feel), Static with product movement (unboxing, demo) |
| **Lighting** | Brightens noticeably (shift from problem scenes), Warm tones (hope, comfort), Clean studio light (clarity, professionalism) |
| **Mood** | Relief, clarity, excitement, possibility |

**Production notes:**
- Visual shift from problem scenes must be noticeable (lighting, color, energy)
- The product appears here — not before
- Show the mechanism or the result, not just the product sitting there
- This is where most watch-time is won or lost

---

### Scene E — Proof/Evidence Scene
**Purpose:** Back the claim. Show that the solution actually works.

| Parameter | Options |
|---|---|
| **Duration** | 4-10 seconds |
| **Camera angle** | Screen recording (dashboard/results), Medium shot (testimonial/user), Close-up (specific result detail) |
| **Camera movement** | Static or slow pan across proof (let them read/absorb), Screen scroll (showing metrics), Cut between multiple proof points |
| **Lighting** | Neutral/natural (authentic, not staged), Screen glow (for screen recordings), Consistent with solution scene |
| **Mood** | Credibility, trust, reassurance, satisfaction |

**Production notes:**
- Numbers must be readable on mobile (large text, high contrast)
- Multiple brief proof points > one long testimonial
- Cut rhythm: 2-3 seconds per proof point to maintain pace

---

### Scene F — CTA Scene
**Purpose:** Tell the viewer exactly what to do next. Remove friction.

| Parameter | Options |
|---|---|
| **Duration** | 2-5 seconds |
| **Camera angle** | Medium shot (person delivering CTA), Product close-up + text overlay, Clean frame with text only |
| **Camera movement** | Static (clarity and focus), Slight push-in (urgency), Pull back to reveal full offer |
| **Lighting** | Bright, clean (clarity), Brand colors prominent, High contrast text areas |
| **Mood** | Confidence, urgency (if scarcity), invitation, clarity |

**Production notes:**
- CTA text must be visible for the full duration (not a flash)
- Verbal CTA + text CTA simultaneously for maximum impact
- End on a clean, stable frame — not a cut to black

## Scene Sequence Templates

## Camera Angle Quick Reference

| Angle | Effect | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| Close-up | Intimacy, emotion, detail | Hooks, pain scenes, CTAs |
| Medium shot | Relatable, conversational | Talking head, testimonial, UGC |
| Wide shot | Context, environment | Lifestyle, establishing shots |
| Low angle | Power, authority, aspiration | Product hero, founder scenes |
| High angle | Vulnerability, being overwhelmed | Problem/agitation scenes |
| Dutch angle | Tension, something is wrong | Pattern interrupt hooks |
| POV / Over-shoulder | Identification, "you are here" | Demo, screen recording, UGC |
