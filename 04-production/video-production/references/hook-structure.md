# Video Hook Structure — 4 Layers

A video hook is not one thing — it's four simultaneous layers working together in the first 1-3 seconds. If any layer fails, the scroll continues.

**Usage rule:** Design all 4 layers BEFORE production. A strong text hook on a weak visual opening = wasted potential. Every layer must earn its place.

---

## The 4 Hook Layers

### Layer 1 — Visual Hook (What they SEE)

**What it does:** Stops the thumb. The visual hook is processed before any text or audio — the brain evaluates visual information in ~13ms. If the first frame doesn't create visual friction, nothing else matters.

**Best practices:**
- Movement in the first frame (hand gesture, object entering frame, camera push)
- Unexpected visual element (unusual color, pattern interrupt, out-of-context object)
- Face close-up (human faces trigger automatic attention — especially eyes and expressions)
- High contrast between subject and background
- Breaking the "ad look" — native content aesthetics stop more scrolls than polished production

**Common mistakes:**
- Static opening frame with no movement (looks like a thumbnail, not a video)
- Logo or brand intro as the first thing shown (screams "ad" — instant scroll)
- Slow zoom or fade-in (too gentle — the feed is aggressive)
- Dark or low-contrast opening (blends into the feed)

**By format:**

| Format | Visual hook approach |
|---|---|
| UGC / Selfie | Person already mid-gesture or mid-sentence. No "Hey guys" intros. |
| Talking Head | Close-up, strong eye contact, leaning into camera. Energy from frame 1. |
| Motion / B-Roll | Fast cut or unexpected object. Movement within the first 0.5s. |
| Screen Recording | Mouse already moving, interface already visible. No loading screens. |
| AI-Generated | Surreal or striking visual that breaks feed norms. Leverage AI's strength = unusual imagery. |

---

### Layer 2 — Audio Hook (What they HEAR)

**What it does:** Captures attention for those with sound on (~30-40% of feed viewers). Creates emotional tone and pacing. Even with sound off, the audio hook matters — it reinforces for the sound-on audience and drives higher watch time.

**Best practices:**
- First word within 0.3 seconds — no silence, no "um", no throat clear
- Voice tone match: energy level must match the hook's emotional register
- Sound effect as pattern interrupt (snap, notification ding, record scratch)
- Music that starts on a strong beat, not a fade-in
- Contrast: silence → sudden voice can be more powerful than constant sound

**Common mistakes:**
- Silence in the first second (sound-on viewers think the video is broken)
- Generic royalty-free music intro (feels like every other ad)
- "Hey guys, so today I wanted to talk about..." (kills all urgency)
- Background music louder than voice (content lost, viewer leaves)

**By format:**

| Format | Audio hook approach |
|---|---|
| UGC / Selfie | Voice starts immediately. Mid-thought opening. "The biggest mistake I made was..." |
| Talking Head | First sentence IS the hook. No preamble. Delivered with conviction. |
| Motion / B-Roll | Sound design first — impactful music beat or SFX. VO enters at 1-2s. |
| Screen Recording | VO starts before the screen is fully visible. Narration drives, visual follows. |
| AI-Generated | AI voice (ElevenLabs) or music-driven. Voice must not sound robotic. |

---

### Layer 3 — Text Overlay Hook (What they READ)

**What it does:** Captures the ~60-70% of viewers scrolling with sound off. The text overlay is often the ONLY hook that reaches the majority of your audience. It must work independently of audio.

**Best practices:**
- Readable in under 2 seconds on mobile (max 8-10 words)
- Positioned in the top 1/3 of the frame (above the thumb zone)
- High contrast against background (white text on dark, dark text on light, or text box)
- Animated entrance (slide-in, pop, or typewriter) — static text blends into the frame
- Must convey the core hook — not just a subtitle of what's being said

**Common mistakes:**
- Text that's too small (unreadable on mobile without squinting)
- Text at the bottom of the frame (covered by platform UI elements)
- Text that's just a transcription of the VO (redundant — use it to ADD information)
- No text at all (you lose 60%+ of your audience immediately)
- Too many words (if it takes more than 2s to read, they've scrolled past)

**By format:**

| Format | Text overlay approach |
|---|---|
| UGC / Selfie | Bold text above the person's head. Different from what they're saying (complementary, not duplicate). |
| Talking Head | Key phrase highlighted. The "scroll-stopper" sentence pulled out and enlarged. |
| Motion / B-Roll | Text IS the primary content layer. Full message communicated through text. |
| Screen Recording | Text at top explaining what they're about to see. "Watch what happens when..." |
| AI-Generated | Text grounds the surreal visual in a relatable message. Bridges AI aesthetic → human relevance. |

---

### Layer 4 — First Frame Composition (The Thumbnail Test)

**What it does:** In autoplay-off environments and in feed previews, the first frame IS the ad. It must work as a still image. Even in autoplay feeds, the first frame sets the visual context before motion is perceived.

**Best practices:**
- The first frame should tell a micro-story on its own (who, what situation, what energy)
- Subject's face visible and emotionally expressive
- Clear visual hierarchy — one focal point, not a cluttered frame
- Brand color or recognizable visual element present but not dominant
- Test: screenshot the first frame — would you stop scrolling on this image alone?

**Common mistakes:**
- First frame is a blur or mid-transition (no visual anchor)
- First frame is black or a title card (zero information to process)
- First frame is identical to every other ad in the category
- Subject facing away from camera or partially out of frame (no connection point)

**By format:**

| Format | First frame composition |
|---|---|
| UGC / Selfie | Person mid-expression (surprise, intensity, concern). Not smiling-to-camera. |
| Talking Head | Close-up, direct eye contact, mouth slightly open (implies speech is happening). |
| Motion / B-Roll | The most visually striking moment of the sequence. Not the establishing shot. |
| Screen Recording | Interface already visible with a compelling data point or result shown. |
| AI-Generated | The most surreal/striking frame. Use AI's visual power upfront. |

---

## Layer Integration Checklist

Before production, verify each hook layer:

| Layer | Question | Pass criteria |
|---|---|---|
| Visual | Does the first frame stop the scroll on its own? | Yes — distinct from feed, movement present |
| Audio | Does the first sound create engagement for sound-on viewers? | Yes — voice or SFX within 0.3s |
| Text | Can a sound-off viewer understand the hook in under 2 seconds? | Yes — readable, positioned correctly, compelling |
| First Frame | Does a screenshot of frame 1 work as a still ad? | Yes — clear subject, emotional expression, visual hierarchy |

---

## Hook Failure Diagnosis

| Symptom | Likely failing layer | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Low Hook Rate (< 20%) | Visual + First Frame | Change the opening visual. Test a more disruptive first frame. |
| High Hook Rate but low watch time | Audio + Text | The visual stops them but the content doesn't retain. Stronger VO and text. |
| Good engagement, low CTR | Text (CTA layer) | The hook works but the message doesn't drive action. Revise text overlay CTA. |
| Inconsistent results across placements | Text (sizing) | Text readable on Feed but not on Reels/Stories. Test placement-specific text sizing. |
