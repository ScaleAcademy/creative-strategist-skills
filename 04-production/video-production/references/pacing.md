# Video Pacing — Rhythm, Transitions, Subtitles

Pacing is the invisible architecture of a video ad. It controls attention, emotion, and retention. Too slow = scroll. Too fast = confusion. The right rhythm matches the viewer's cognitive processing speed for each content type.

---

## Cut Rhythm by Duration

### Short-Form (6-15 seconds)

| Element | Guideline |
|---|---|
| Average cut length | 1.5-2.5 seconds |
| Total cuts | 4-8 |
| Hook duration | 1-2 seconds (must grab within first second) |
| Body | 3-10 seconds — one idea, one proof point |
| CTA | 1-3 seconds — text overlay or verbal |
| Pacing feel | Punchy, relentless. No dead space. Every frame earns its place. |

**Best for:** TOF awareness, Reels/TikTok, pattern interrupt, single-message creatives.

### Mid-Form (15-45 seconds)

| Element | Guideline |
|---|---|
| Average cut length | 2-4 seconds |
| Total cuts | 6-15 |
| Hook duration | 2-3 seconds |
| Body | 10-35 seconds — 2-3 points or one story arc |
| CTA | 2-5 seconds |
| Pacing feel | Breathing room between points, but no filler. Each section has its own mini-hook. |

**Best for:** MOF consideration, product demos, case studies, how-to content.

### Long-Form (45 seconds - 3 minutes)

| Element | Guideline |
|---|---|
| Average cut length | 3-6 seconds |
| Total cuts | 10-30 |
| Hook duration | 3-5 seconds (can include a pre-hook teaser) |
| Body | 30-150 seconds — full argument with multiple sections |
| CTA | 5-10 seconds (can include reinforcement) |
| Pacing feel | Narrative flow. Varies between fast sections (proof, energy) and slow sections (emotion, story). |

**Best for:** BOF conversion, VSLs, founder stories, testimonial compilations.

## Pacing by Format

| Format | Pace | Cut Style | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| F45 Yapper | Fast | Minimal cuts (1-3 for emphasis) | Energy comes from delivery, not editing |
| F06 Case Study | Medium | Section-based cuts | Each section has its own visual world |
| F10 Demo | Medium-fast | Action-driven cuts | Cut on the action — click, swipe, result |
| F03 Before/After | Variable | Slow before → fast after | Contrast in pacing mirrors the transformation |
| F33 Social Proof Mashup | Very fast | 2-4 seconds per testimonial | Volume of proof = fast pace |
| F08 Cinematic B-Roll | Slow-medium | Long holds, smooth transitions | Let the visual breathe. Music drives the rhythm. |
| F32 Skit | Fast | Comedy timing cuts | Cut on the punchline or reaction |

## Transition Types

| Transition | When to use | When NOT to use |
|---|---|---|
| **Hard cut** | Default. 90% of transitions. Clean, fast, professional. | Never wrong — it's the safest choice. |
| **Jump cut** | Yapper/talking head — removes pauses and filler. Creates energy. | Cinematic content, emotional storytelling. |
| **Match cut** | Linking two related visuals (same shape, same motion). Very satisfying. | When the match isn't precise — a weak match cut looks like a mistake. |
| **Swipe/slide** | Listicle slides, section transitions, carousel-feel in video. | More than 2-3 per video. Overuse feels like a slideshow. |
| **Zoom punch** | Emphasis on a key word or moment. Energy boost. | Overuse (more than 2 per video = TikTok cliche). |
| **L-cut / J-cut** | Audio from next scene starts before the visual (J) or continues after (L). Smooth narrative flow. | Fast-paced edits where clean cuts work better. |
| **Fade to black** | End of video. Major section break. Emotional moment. | Mid-video — it signals "the end" and viewers leave. |
| **Whip pan** | Energy transition between locations or time jumps. | Slow, emotional content. Overuse = nausea. |

## Animated Subtitles

### Style Options

| Style | Look | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **Word-by-word highlight** | Each word lights up as spoken. TikTok/Reels native. | UGC, Yapper, fast-paced content |
| **Phrase blocks** | 3-5 word phrases appear as blocks. Readable, clean. | Talking head, expert content, longer videos |
| **Keyword pop** | Only key words animate (scale up, color change, bold). Rest stays static. | Emphasis on specific terms, educational content |
| **Karaoke style** | Full sentence visible, current word highlighted. | Storytelling, emotional content |

### Subtitle Best Practices

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| Position | Center-bottom for Feed. Center for Reels/Stories (avoid platform UI zones). |
| Font size | Minimum 40pt equivalent on mobile. Test on a real phone. |
| Max words per screen | 6-8 words. More = unreadable at feed scroll speed. |
| Background | Semi-transparent box or text shadow. Never raw text on busy backgrounds. |
| Color | White on dark, black on light. One accent color for emphasis words. |
| Animation timing | Sync to audio within 100ms. Delayed subtitles feel broken. |

## Music Sync Points

| Video moment | Music sync |
|---|---|
| Hook (0-3s) | Beat drop or strong note on frame 1. Silence → sound also works. |
| Key claim or stat | Music dip (lower volume) so the voice cuts through. |
| Transition between sections | Beat or rhythmic change to signal shift. |
| Emotional peak | Music swells. Voice may pause to let music carry the emotion. |
| CTA | Music simplifies or drops to create focus on the action. |
| Final frame | Clean ending — music resolves, doesn't fade out awkwardly mid-phrase. |

## Pacing Diagnosis

| Symptom | Likely cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| High Hook Rate, low average watch time | Body pacing too slow after hook | Tighten cuts in the first 10s after hook |
| Good watch time, low CTR | CTA section too rushed or buried | Extend CTA to 3-5s, add text overlay reinforcement |
| Viewers drop at specific timestamp | That section is too long or unclear | Cut it shorter or add a visual change at that point |
| Low engagement on Reels but good on Feed | Pacing too slow for vertical format | Speed up cuts by 30-40% for Reels versions |
