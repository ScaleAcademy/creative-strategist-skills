# Pattern Library — Winning Patterns (Living Document)

A living library of recurring patterns observed in high-performing ad creatives. Each pattern is documented with context, metrics, and a testable hypothesis.

**Usage rule:** This document grows over time. When you spot a pattern across 3+ winning ads, add it here. Patterns are hypotheses until validated by your own data.

---

## How to Use This Library

1. **Before briefing:** Scan patterns relevant to your awareness stage and format
2. **After analysis:** When a creative wins, check if it matches a known pattern — or document a new one
3. **For testing:** Each pattern includes a test recommendation — use it to validate the pattern in your account

---

## Pattern Entry Structure

Each pattern follows this format:
- **Pattern name** — Short, memorable label
- **Description** — What it is and why it works
- **Where spotted** — Platforms, industries, accounts
- **Metrics signal** — What metrics indicate this pattern is present and working
- **Hypothesis** — Why this works psychologically
- **Recommended test** — How to isolate and validate this pattern

## Documented Patterns

### PAT-01 — The 0.5s Face
**Description:** Video opens with a human face filling 60%+ of the frame for the first 0.5 seconds before any text, logo, or context appears. The face shows a strong emotion (surprise, concern, intensity).
**Where spotted:** Meta Reels, TikTok. DTC brands, coaching, SaaS. Works across industries.
**Metrics signal:** Hook Rate > 35%, significantly higher than same-body ads with non-face openings.
**Hypothesis:** Human faces trigger automatic attention (fusiform face area). Emotion on the face creates instant curiosity about WHY they feel that way. The brain must stay to find out.
**Recommended test:** Take your best-performing video. Create version A with current hook and version B with a 0.5s face-first cold open. Same body, same CTA. Test Hook Rate and 3s view rate.

---

### PAT-02 — The Native Ugly
**Description:** Deliberately low-production creative that looks like organic content — shaky phone footage, imperfect lighting, no branding. Outperforms polished studio content at TOF.
**Where spotted:** Meta Feed, TikTok, Instagram Stories. Fitness, info products, B2C services.
**Metrics signal:** Higher CTR and lower CPA than polished equivalents at TOF. Lower CPM (platform favors native-looking content).
**Hypothesis:** The ad filter is bypassed. Viewers process it as organic content, giving it more attention before the brain categorizes it as advertising. Trust is higher because "real people don't have production budgets."
**Recommended test:** Reshoot your top-performing ad concept on a phone, in natural light, with no branding until the CTA. Compare CPA head-to-head.

---

### PAT-03 — The Specificity Spike
**Description:** Replacing vague claims with hyper-specific numbers dramatically improves performance. "€47,283 in 62 days" beats "tens of thousands in a few months."
**Where spotted:** Universal — all platforms, all industries. Especially powerful in Meta Feed.
**Metrics signal:** Higher CTR on specific-number variants. Higher conversion rate on landing pages with specific proof.
**Hypothesis:** Specificity signals truthfulness. Vague numbers feel like marketing. Precise numbers feel like real data. The brain trusts precision because lying precisely is cognitively harder.
**Recommended test:** Take 5 ads with vague metrics. Replace with exact numbers. Run as A/B test. Measure CTR and conversion rate.

---

### PAT-04 — The Comment-First Hook
**Description:** Video opens by showing a real (or curated) social media comment, then the creator responds to it. The comment contains a common objection or question.
**Where spotted:** TikTok, Instagram Reels. Training, coaching, SaaS, e-commerce.
**Metrics signal:** Hook Rate > 30%, high comment rate on the ad itself, strong share rate.
**Hypothesis:** The comment format is deeply native to social platforms. Viewers process it as a conversation, not an ad. The objection in the comment mirrors their own doubt — creating instant relevance.
**Recommended test:** Collect top 5 objections from your audience research. Create comment-response videos for each. Test which objection drives the highest Hook Rate.

---

### PAT-05 — The Silent Text
**Description:** Video with no voice-over, no music — just animated text on a visual background. Text appears word-by-word or phrase-by-phrase. Outperforms VO versions in certain contexts.
**Where spotted:** Meta Feed (sound-off default), Instagram Stories. Works for simple messages.
**Metrics signal:** Higher completion rate than VO equivalents in Feed placement. Lower production cost, faster iteration.
**Hypothesis:** In a sound-off feed, text-only content requires no adaptation from the viewer. There's no "should I turn on sound?" friction. The message is received immediately and completely.
**Recommended test:** Take your best VO ad. Create a text-only version with the same message. Run both in Feed placement only. Compare completion rate and CPA.

---

### PAT-06 — The Contrarian Open
**Description:** First sentence contradicts a widely held belief in the target market. "Stop optimizing for ROAS." "The more you spend on ads, the less you earn." Triggers pattern interrupt + curiosity.
**Where spotted:** Meta Feed, LinkedIn, YouTube pre-roll. B2B, training, expertise-based products.
**Metrics signal:** Very high Hook Rate (35%+) but watch-time dependent on whether the contrarian claim is backed up. If not backed up, high drop-off at 5-10s.
**Hypothesis:** Contradiction of existing beliefs triggers the brain's error-detection system. The viewer MUST stay to resolve the cognitive dissonance. But the claim must be defensible — unresolved dissonance creates frustration, not conversion.
**Recommended test:** Identify 3 common beliefs in your market. Write contrarian hooks that challenge each one. Pair each with a strong evidence body. Test Hook Rate AND average watch time together.
